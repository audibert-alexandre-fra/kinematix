# Kinematix

### Open-source system for clinical motion analysis using deep learning

---

## 👋 Overview

Kinematix is an open-source project developed over two years by a team including two engineers and a physiotherapist.

The goal of the project is to provide an easy-to-deploy camera-based system for human motion analysis in clinical settings. It enables physiotherapists to study patient movements such as walking, squats, and running, and to obtain accurate quantitative metrics to support rehabilitation.

---

## 🧠 Core idea

The system leverages recent advances in deep learning and optimization to analyze human motion from a small number of standard cameras.

Only 2 to 4 network-connected cameras are required, with **no explicit camera calibration needed**, significantly simplifying deployment in real clinical environments.

---

## ⚙️ Performance

With a standard workstation, a 15-second motion sequence can be processed in approximately 2–3 minutes.

The system provides detailed motion analysis and multiple quantitative metrics to assist clinical decision-making.

---

## 🏃 Motion types

The system has been evaluated on three main types of movements:

- Walking
- Squats
- Running

For each of these, Kinematix produces robust motion descriptors and clinically relevant metrics.

---

## 💻 Interface

Kinematix includes a simple and user-friendly web interface designed for physiotherapists, enabling easy interaction with the system and visualization of results.
